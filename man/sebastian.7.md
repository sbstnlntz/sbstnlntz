<h2>NAME</h2>
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;sebastian - developer philosophy, mindset and principles
</p>

<h2>DESCRIPTION</h2>
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This manual describes my philosophy as a developer. It focuses on<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;how I think, learn and approach software creation.
</p>

<h2>CORE PRINCIPLES</h2>
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Clarity over cleverness – my future self must understand my present self.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Simplicity as a strategy – complexity grows by itself.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Strong foundations – good architecture stays invisible.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Intentional design – UI should feel obvious, logic should feel honest.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Curiosity as fuel – learning never stops.
</p>

<h2>WORKFLOW PHILOSOPHY</h2>
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Start small. Move, measure, adjust.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leave breadcrumbs – comments explain decisions.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Build for tomorrow – code must survive refactors.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fail early – errors are information.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ship often – small releases hurt less.
</p>

<h2>MINDSET</h2>
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Calm over chaos.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adaptability over rigidity.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ownership of everything I touch.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Balance leads to better code.
</p>
